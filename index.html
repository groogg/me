My hobbit journey

Studied data science, got a master's, and realized they forgot to teach programming. Decided to learn it myself.

Joined Ivado Labs in 2020 as a pseudo-data scientist. Worked hard, moved to the official title, and then to science lead. Missed the deterministic nature of software engineering, so I decided I wanted to jump over the fence.

Worked hard and moved to Team Lead. Now, I can do both and focus on ensuring our teams are happy, growing, and motivated to deliver.

Happy since.

Things I've worked on

Airline pricing - Applied new ML-powered pricing that focused on maximizing revenue and data variability. Sad to use Azure, and realized how expensive Snowflake was, but good networking challenges.

Retail promotion optimization - Optimized the promotional calendar of a catalogue of 2000+ SKUs for the next quarter. The forecasting model was challenging, but the optimization worked remarkably well.

Agricultural precision forecasting - Reduced the required number of on-field sensors to predict in-canopy conditions by combining forecasting and sensor data. Now, I know too much about how larvae evolve to eat your leaves. Working with satellite imagery was a great check on my bucket list.

Luckily, I’ve got 10+ others just like that.

Current stack

Not rigid on the stack—cloud, on-prem, monolith, or distributed. I just care about elegance, simplicity, and solving the problem at hand.
